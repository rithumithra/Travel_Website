About Hampi

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is renowned for its ancient ruins, temples, and rich history. Located in the state of Karnataka, India, Hampi offers a glimpse into the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Historical Significance

Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century and flourished as one of the richest and largest cities in the world during that time. The ruins of Hampi offer insights into its glorious past. Hampi-Vijayanagara was the world's second-largest city, after Beijing, and probably India's richest at that time, attracting traders from Persia and Portugal.

Major Tourist Attractions

Some of the major tourist attractions in Hampi include the Virupaksha Temple, Hampi Bazaar, Vijaya Vittala Temple, Lotus Mahal, and the iconic Hampi Stone Chariot. Other highlights include the Lotus Mahal, the Elephant Stables, and the serene Tungabhadra River. Hampi's rich history and stunning landscapes, including Matanga Hill and the Hemakuta Hill Temple Complex, make it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Hampi is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is pleasant for exploring the historical sites and monuments.

Things to Do in Hampi

  • Visit the Virupaksha Temple:Pay homage to Lord Shiva at this ancient temple
  • Witness the Hampi Bazaar:bustling with street vendors selling traditional handicrafts and souvenirs
